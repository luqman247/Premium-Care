# 01 · Brand Story

Denmark has one of the world's most admired care traditions. It is built on a simple conviction: that how a society treats its elderly and its vulnerable is the truest measure of its character.

Premium Care exists to raise that standard further.

We were founded on the belief that care in the home should never feel like a compromise — that the person receiving care in their own living room deserves the same excellence, discretion and attention to detail found in Denmark's finest institutions. Not care that is merely delivered, but care that is *composed*: planned with clinical rigour, performed with human warmth, and carried out with the quiet confidence of true professionals.

Our name is a promise, not a boast. *Premium* describes the standard we hold ourselves to. *Care* describes everything we do. And our strapline — **Omsorg • Tryghed • Hver Dag** — describes how it feels to be one of our clients: cared for, safe, every single day.

The shield in our mark carries the Dannebrog, the world's oldest national flag, because our standard is a national one. The two figures within it — one supporting the other — are the entire company in a single image. The crown above signals not royalty, but the sovereign dignity of every person in our care.

**The story in one sentence:** *Premium Care brings Denmark's highest standard of care into the place people most want to receive it — their own home.*

# 02 · Mission, Vision, Purpose

**Purpose** *(why we exist)*
To ensure that growing older, living with illness or needing support never costs a person their dignity, their safety or the comfort of home.

**Mission** *(what we do)*
We deliver home care, nursing, rehabilitation and specialist care of uncompromising quality — combining clinical excellence with genuine human warmth, for private clients and municipalities across Denmark.

**Vision** *(where we are going)*
To be Denmark's most trusted premium home care provider — the name families recommend without hesitation and municipalities engage without reservation.

**A note on hierarchy:** Purpose is permanent. Mission may evolve with services. Vision is reviewed every five years. None may be altered without executive approval under the governance procedure in §21.

# 03 · Values

Our values are expressed first in Danish, because they are lived in Danish — in homes, in conversations, in care plans. The English definitions exist so that every partner, agency and supplier understands precisely what each value demands.

**1. Værdighed — Dignity**
The client is a person first and a patient second. We knock before we enter. We ask before we act. We speak *with*, never *about*. Every process, product and photograph must pass one test: does it honour the dignity of the person in our care?

**2. Tryghed — Security**
Trust is built through predictability. The same faces, the agreed times, the promised standard. Tryghed is the feeling our brand must produce in every touchpoint — from the reassurance of a uniform to the clarity of an invoice.

**3. Nærvær — Presence**
Care cannot be rushed and remain care. We are fully present in every visit: attentive, unhurried in manner, generous with eye contact and listening. In brand terms: we never shout, we never clutter, we give every element room.

**4. Faglighed — Professional Excellence**
Warmth without competence is sentiment; competence without warmth is service. We insist on both. Our people are rigorously qualified, continuously trained and clinically current. Our communications are precise, correct and never careless.

**5. Ordentlighed — Decency**
A deeply Danish word with no perfect translation: doing things properly, honestly and thoroughly — especially when no one is watching. In practice: transparent pricing, honest reporting to municipalities, promises kept, mistakes owned.

**Behavioural rule:** every value must be demonstrable. If a page of the manual, a photograph or a campaign cannot be traced back to at least one value, it does not belong in the brand.

# 04 · Brand Personality

If Premium Care were a person, they would be a senior Danish nurse with the composure of a diplomat: calm, immaculately prepared, quietly warm, incapable of condescension.

| Dimension | We are | We are never |
|---|---|---|
| Warmth | Warm, human, attentive | Sentimental, gushing, pitying |

# 05 · Tone of Voice

**The principle: warm precision.** Every sentence should feel like it was written by someone who is both highly competent and genuinely kind.

**Five rules of the Premium Care voice:**

1. **Write to one person.** Not "clients receive", but "you receive". Not "relatives can", but "your family can".
2. **Short sentences carry trust.** If a sentence needs a second breath, it needs an edit.
3. **Plain words for hard subjects.** We say *dementia*, *palliative care*, *end of life* — clearly, gently and without euphemism. Euphemism signals discomfort; clarity signals experience.
4. **Never sell fear.** We do not market anxiety about ageing. We speak to the solution, never dramatise the problem.
5. **Danish dignity in every language.** British English for international and corporate materials; Danish for clients, care documentation and municipal work. The strapline remains in Danish everywhere, without exception.

**Voice in three registers:**

- *To families:* "We will be there at eight, as always. And we will stay as long as your mother needs us."
- *To municipalities:* "Our documentation is complete, our compliance record is public, and our pricing contains no surprises."
- *To colleagues:* "You represent the highest standard in Danish care. Dress, speak and document accordingly."

**Forbidden vocabulary:** *world-class, solutions, passion/passionate, journey (metaphorical), loved ones (use "your family" / "the people close to you"), state-of-the-art, care packages (use "care plans"), users (use "clients" or names).*

# 06 · Target Audiences

**Primary — The Adult Daughter or Son (45–65).** The true decision-maker in most private engagements. Time-poor, quality-conscious, carrying quiet guilt about not doing more themselves. They are buying certainty and peace of mind. Message: *you have chosen the best; you can rest now.*

**Secondary — The Client (70+).** Often sceptical of "help", protective of independence and home. Message: *this is your home, your routine, your life — we fit around you, with respect.*

**Tertiary — Municipal Procurement & Visitation Teams.** Rational buyers governed by frit valg (free choice) frameworks, compliance and documentation. Message: *premium standards, procurement-grade transparency.*

**Quaternary — Care Professionals (recruitment).** Nurses, SOSU assistants and therapists choosing between employers. Message: *work where the standard matches your pride in your profession.*

**Strategic note:** the identity must perform simultaneously in a family living room and a municipal tender portal. This dual audience is the central design constraint of the entire programme — and the reason for the *quiet premium* doctrine in §04.

# 07 · Positioning & Brand Promise

**Category:** Premium private home care and healthcare, Denmark.

**Positioning statement:**
*For families and municipalities who will not compromise on care, Premium Care is the Danish home care provider that combines clinical excellence with genuine human warmth — delivered to the highest standard in the country, every day.*

**Competitive frame:**
- Versus **municipal providers**: we match their legitimacy and exceed their consistency, choice and attentiveness.
- Versus **large private groups (Aleris, Falck ecosystems)**: we exceed their intimacy, continuity of carers, and premium finish.
- Versus **small local providers**: we exceed their professionalism, documentation and reliability at scale.

**The brand promise:**
> **Omsorg • Tryghed • Hver Dag**
> Care. Security. Every day.

This is a contract, not a slogan. *Omsorg* is the warmth, *Tryghed* is the reliability, *Hver Dag* is the consistency. Every chapter of the manual, and every service visit, is measured against these three words.

# 08 · Brand Architecture

**Strategy: a monolithic master brand (branded house).**

All services live under the single Premium Care identity. Service lines are descriptors, never sub-brands:

- Premium Care · Hjemmepleje (Home Care)
- Premium Care · Sygepleje (Nursing)
- Premium Care · Demensomsorg (Dementia Care)
- Premium Care · Fysioterapi (Physiotherapy)
- Premium Care · Ergoterapi (Occupational Therapy)
- Premium Care · Rehabilitering (Rehabilitation)
- Premium Care · Palliativ Omsorg (Palliative Care)
- Premium Care · Praktisk Hjælp (Practical Support)

**Rules:**
1. No service line receives its own logo, colour or typeface. Differentiation is achieved through the icon suite (Manual, Part 05) only.
2. The naming pattern is fixed: *Premium Care · [Service]*, set in the brand typography with the gold interpunct.
3. Future ventures (e.g. private clinics, senior residences) must be approved through governance (§21) and default to the same master-brand pattern.

**Rationale:** trust in care is not divisible. Every visit, vehicle and invoice must deposit equity into a single account.

---

# 09 · Messaging Principles

1. **Lead with reassurance, prove with rigour.** Every message opens with the human benefit and closes with the professional evidence.
2. **One idea per communication.** A page, advertisement or post carries a single message. Density is the enemy of trust.
3. **The strapline is a signature, not a headline.** *Omsorg • Tryghed • Hver Dag* appears in lockup with the logo or as a closing line — never as body copy, never rearranged, never translated.
4. **Names over numbers in human contexts; numbers over adjectives in municipal contexts.**
5. **We never disparage competitors or the public system.** Premium Care positions *above*, never *against*. Denmark's public care system is a partner and a client, not a foil.

**Master messages (approved formulations):**
- *Denmark's highest standard of care, at home.*
- *The same faces. The agreed times. The promised standard.*
- *Care planned like medicine. Delivered like family.* (private audiences only)
- *Premium standards. Procurement-grade transparency.* (municipal audiences only)

# 10 · Logo Philosophy

The approved Premium Care mark is a heraldic composition of four elements, each with fixed meaning:

**The Shield.** Protection — the fundamental promise of care. It frames everything we do.

**The Dannebrog Cross.** The Danish standard, in both senses: our nationality and our benchmark. It anchors the brand in Danish care tradition and signals legitimacy to municipal partners.

**The Two Figures.** One person steadying another — support given at eye level, between equals. Never carer above client. This is the brand's entire ethic in silhouette, and the reason the mark must never be redrawn casually.

**The Crown.** The sovereign dignity of the person in care, and the standard of craftsmanship we borrow from the finest Danish houses. It is an emblem of standards, not status.

**The Wordmark and Strapline.** A refined serif wordmark with the Danish strapline beneath, separated by gold interpuncts — an editorial signature that completes the mark.

**Doctrinal rules (expanded in Manual, Part 02):**
1. The logo is never redrawn, re-proportioned, re-coloured outside approved palettes, or combined with other marks.
2. The gold rendering is reserved for premium contexts (navy grounds, foil, embroidery, vehicles). Single-colour navy is the workhorse for documents.
3. The shield-only, monogram (PC) and crown favicon are *supporting* marks: they may appear only where the full logo has already established the brand or where space is technically constrained.
4. On municipal tender documents the full formal lockup is mandatory.

**Secondary palette (functional):** a warm grey scale for documentation, a muted sage for wellbeing contexts, a soft sky for digital information states — specified fully in Manual, Part 03.

**The proportion doctrine — 60 / 30 / 8 / 2:**
Porcelain 60 · Navy 30 · Gold 8 · Red 2. Red is precious: it lives in the shield and is not a general accent colour. Gold is seasoning, not sauce. Any layout that inverts these proportions reads as costume, not confidence.

**Accessibility commitment:** all text/background pairings must meet WCAG 2.2 AA. Gold is never used for body text on white. Full contrast matrix in Manual, Part 03.

# 12 · Typography Philosophy & Recommendation

Typography will do more daily work for Premium Care than any other element: care plans, tenders, the website, a business card handed over at a kitchen table. The system must be simultaneously *elegant* (private audiences), *legible* (older readers), *compliant* (accessibility and tenders) and *practical* (Word, PowerPoint, email).

## The evaluation

Three pairings were assessed against the ten criteria set by the client (Scandinavian elegance, luxury positioning, healthcare professionalism, readability, accessibility, digital performance, corporate documentation, tender submissions, web usability, print quality).

**Option A — Berlingske Serif + Berlingske Sans (Playtype, Copenhagen)**
A contemporary Danish superfamily designed by the Copenhagen foundry Playtype, originally commissioned for Denmark's oldest newspaper. The serif is dignified and quietly luxurious with superb Danish diacritics (æ, ø, å drawn natively, not adapted); the sans is humanist, warm and highly legible at small sizes. Serif and sans share skeletal proportions, so mixed pages feel engineered rather than assembled. Weight range is exceptional (including a Slab and Mono for data). Web font performance is strong; desktop licensing is straightforward.
*Strengths:* authentically Danish provenance — a genuine brand story, not a styling choice; single-foundry harmony; excellent at both display and caption sizes.
*Risks:* association with the Berlingske newspaper for some Danish readers — minor, and diminishing as the family is now widely licensed.

**Option B — Tiempos (Text/Headline) + Söhne (Klim Type Foundry)**
The international premium-editorial standard. Impeccable craftsmanship, flawless screen rendering.
*Strengths:* world-class quality; strong luxury signal.
*Risks:* no Danish story; Söhne's ubiquity in tech branding slightly erodes distinctiveness; two families with different DNA require more art direction to harmonise.

**Option C — Freight Text + Aktiv Grotesk**
The safe corporate route. Robust, licensed everywhere, entirely competent.
*Strengths:* low risk, wide availability.
*Risks:* no point of view; reads as "professional services generic". Fails the *never generic* brief.

**Rejected directions, for the record:** Didone serifs (Didot/Bodoni) — fashion-luxury coding, poor legibility for older readers at text sizes. Geometric sans (Futura-alikes, Circular) — over-used in Nordic startups; the opposite of timeless. Optima-style humanist glyphics — indelibly associated with 1990s healthcare and cosmetics.

## The recommendation

**Adopt Option A: the Berlingske superfamily.**

- **Berlingske Serif** — brand voice: wordmark contexts, headlines, pull statements, executive documents, the manual itself.
- **Berlingske Sans** — working voice: body copy in clinical and municipal documents, UI, wayfinding, forms, captions.
- **Berlingske Mono** (limited) — tabular data in tenders and reports.

The justification is strategic, not merely aesthetic: *Premium Care's entire positioning is Danish excellence, and its typography can be literally Danish.* Designed in Copenhagen, drawn around Danish letterforms, tested daily by Danish readers for over a decade. No competitor in Danish care can claim this. It converts a licensing decision into brand equity.

**Mandatory fallback stack (non-negotiable for tenders and Office templates):**
- Serif contexts → Georgia → Times New Roman
- Sans contexts → Verdana → Arial
- Digital system stack: `"Berlingske Sans", -apple-system, "Segoe UI", Verdana, sans-serif`

Fallbacks ensure a municipal caseworker opening a Word document without brand fonts still receives a dignified, legible page. Full hierarchy, sizing and licensing schedule follows in Manual, Part 04.

# 13 · Photography Direction

**Philosophy: dignity-first documentary.** Our photography should look *found*, not staged — as if a discreet observer captured a genuinely good moment.

**Three approved categories:**
1. **Relational** — carer and client together, at eye level, mid-conversation or mid-task. Connection, not assistance, is the subject.
2. **Environmental** — Danish homes and light: morning windows, worn wooden floors, coffee cups, gardens. The home as a place worth staying in.
3. **Professional** — uniformed staff, composed and confident; clinical competence shown through preparation and posture, not equipment close-ups.

**The grade:** natural Nordic daylight, slightly warm, gentle contrast, true skin tones. Navy and gold may echo in wardrobe and interiors but are never forced.

**Absolute prohibitions:** stock-photo grins at the camera; clients photographed from above; hands-clasped "comfort" clichés; medical hardware as hero; anyone appearing frail *as the point of the image*; heavy filters; artificial lens flare.

**Ethics (binding):** written consent for every identifiable client; the right to withdraw at any time; no photography during personal care; dementia-care imagery requires next-of-kin consent and executive sign-off. Dignity outranks deadline, always.

# 14 · Graphic Language

Beyond logo, colour and type, four proprietary elements make a page unmistakably Premium Care:

1. **The Gold Keyline.** A 0.5 pt champagne-gold rule used to frame, underline and divide. The brand's signature of craft — used once or twice per page, never as decoration lattice.
2. **The Cross Grid.** A subtle background pattern derived from the shield's cross geometry, used at low contrast on dividers, folder interiors and tender covers.
3. **The Interpunct Signature.** The gold "•" from the strapline, used as a list marker and separator in premium contexts — a small, ownable habit.
4. **The Divider Ornament.** The fine flourish from the strapline lockup, reserved for covers and certificates.

**Layout doctrine:** generous margins, asymmetric balance on the 12-column grid, one hero element per page, white space treated as a material rather than an absence. If a layout feels empty, it is probably correct.

# 15 · Iconography, Illustration & Data

- **Service icon suite (10):** line icons at a fixed stroke, drawn on a shared grid derived from the shield's proportions; navy on light grounds, gold on navy. Human figures in icons follow the logo's principle — support between equals.
- **Illustration:** used sparingly, for explaining processes (e.g. visitation to first visit). Style: fine-line, single-weight, navy with gold accents. Never cartoon, never characterful mascots.
- **Data visualisation:** navy scale bars, gold for the highlighted figure, red never used in charts (reserved for the shield). Typography in Berlingske Sans/Mono. Every chart must be readable in greyscale for tender reproduction.

# 16 · Customer Experience Principles

The brand is delivered by people, not PDFs. Five experience principles bind operations to identity:

1. **Arrive as promised.** Punctuality is the first proof of *Tryghed*.
2. **Known faces.** Continuity of carers is a brand feature, protected in scheduling policy.
3. **The home leads.** We adapt to the client's home, habits and rhythm — never the reverse.
4. **Explain before acting.** Every intervention is narrated in plain Danish first.
5. **Finish beautifully.** Every visit ends with the home as ordered as we found it, and clarity about the next visit.

---

# 17 · Employee Experience Principles

The uniform is the most-seen brand application in the company. The people inside it are the brand's primary medium.

1. **Pride is designed.** Uniforms, ID badges and vehicles must make staff feel like members of Denmark's finest care organisation — recruitment begins with how our people look and feel at work.
2. **Standards are training, not policing.** Every brand rule that touches staff (uniform, tone, documentation) is taught with its reasoning.
3. **The brand speaks internally first.** Internal documents receive the same typographic care as client materials; we do not keep two standards.
4. **Recognition in brand language.** Excellence is celebrated using the values' Danish names — a *Værdighed* commendation means something specific.

# 18 · Design Principles (The Five Tests)

Every artefact — page, screen, sign, stitch — must pass all five before release:

1. **Dignified?** Would the person in our care be proud to see this?
2. **Calm?** Does it reduce anxiety rather than create urgency?
3. **Danish?** Does it feel rooted in Danish craft and restraint?
4. **Durable?** Will it look correct in ten years?
5. **Useful?** Can a printer, developer or caseworker act on it without asking questions?

Failure of any single test returns the work to the studio.

# 21 · Strategic Counsel — Where We Have Challenged the Brief

As instructed, we have acted as partners rather than executors. Three deliberate positions taken in this Bible merit your explicit attention:

**1. "Luxury" has been translated into "quiet premium" (§04).**
Overt luxury coding is a liability in Danish healthcare: it risks alienating municipal buyers governed by public-spend scrutiny, and it can read as exclusivity in a sector defined by universalism. We have therefore encoded premium through *craft, restraint and reliability* — Georg Jensen rather than gold leaf. The gold in the identity is disciplined to 8% of any composition for this reason.

**2. Dannebrog Red has been confined almost entirely to the shield (§11).**
Red is clinically charged (alarms, warnings, blood) and politically charged (flag as decoration). By making it precious — present, proud, but contained — the flag remains an emblem of standard rather than a graphic device. We advise strongly against red as a general accent colour.

**3. Danish-language values (§03) rather than English corporate values.**
*Værdighed, Tryghed, Nærvær, Faglighed, Ordentlighed* are untranslatably Danish, immediately credible to staff and municipalities, and impossible for international competitors to imitate authentically. English definitions travel; the values themselves stay Danish — exactly as you ruled for the strapline.
