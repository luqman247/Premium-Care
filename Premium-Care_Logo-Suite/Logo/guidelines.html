Section 01

Brand Foundation

Omsorg · Tryghed · Hver Dag —
Care, security and dignity, every single day.

Essence

Premium Care is a private Danish home-care institution built on a simple conviction: that the final chapters of life deserve the same excellence, discretion and dignity as any other. The brand carries the visual language of Danish heritage — the Dannebrog cross, the crown, the deep maritime navy — and pairs it with the warmth of two figures meeting face to face, supported and seen.

Positioning

Where public provision offers standardisation, Premium Care offers continuity of carer, unhurried visits and hotel-grade service discipline. The identity must always signal institution, never agency: permanent, accountable and quietly proud.

The Three Values
ValueMeaning in practice
Omsorg
Care		Every interaction is personal. Carers are matched, not dispatched. Attention to the small courtesies — shoes at the door, names remembered, tea made properly.
Tryghed
Security		Predictability is a service feature: the same faces, the same times, documented handovers, and a family who always knows who is in the home.
Hver Dag
Every Day		Excellence is not episodic. The standard on a quiet Tuesday equals the standard on inspection day. Consistency is the promise.
Brand Personality

Dignified · Warm · Danish · Assured · Discreet. Never clinical, never sentimental. The register of a family office that happens to deliver care — closer to Amanruya than to a hospital corridor.

The Mark — Anatomy & Symbolism

ElementMeaning
The CrownRoyal Danish heritage and the standard of service to which the institution holds itself — care worthy of a kingdom’s eldest citizens. The single star above signifies one uncompromising standard.
The ShieldProtection and guardianship. The shield form frames every promise the brand makes: the client is defended — their dignity, their routines, their home.
The DannebrogThe world’s oldest national flag, rendered in heritage red and ivory. Premium Care is unmistakably Danish: local carers, Danish regulatory discipline, Danish values of tillid og tryghed.
The Two FiguresAn elderly gentleman and lady, upright and facing one another — companionship, not dependency. Cast in gold: the people served are the treasure of the institution.
The WordmarkInscriptional Roman capitals in gilded serif, with the Danish device line Omsorg · Tryghed · Hver Dag set in letter-spaced capitals between gold hairlines and diamond points.

The complete mark is a heraldic achievement, and is treated with heraldic discipline: it is never redrawn, re-proportioned, re-coloured or ornamented beyond the approved renditions in this manual.

Primary Lockup — Clear Space & Minimum Size

Clear Space

The exclusion zone equals the height of the crown, measured from its base to the star — the unit x. No typography, imagery, page edge or competing mark may enter this zone on any side.

Section 05

Secondary Lockups

The Emblem

The crest alone. For avatars, favicons, wax seals, embroidery, lapel pins, vehicle marques and any context where the name is already established. The gilded rendition is reserved for ceremonial and premium touchpoints.

The Wordmark

Name and device line without the crest. For narrow horizontal spaces — document footers, web navigation on scroll, spine printing — where the emblem would collapse below its minimum size.

The Horizontal Lockup

Emblem and wordmark side by side. The workhorse for letterheads, email signatures, website headers and co-branded documents. The emblem height defines the lockup; spacing is fixed and supplied as an asset.

Section 10

Graphic Devices & Iconography

The Device Line
OMSORG · TRYGHED · HVER DAG
◆

The tagline construction — letter-spaced capitals between fading gold hairlines, closed by a single diamond — is a brand device in its own right. It may frame section openings, certificates and invitations. The diamond ◆ is the brand’s sole ornament: it separates, it closes, it never decorates in multiples of more than three.

Rules & Frames

Hairline rules at 0.4 pt in Regal Gold, always fading to transparent at one end. The gold hairline frame with centred diamond (as on every page of this manual) marks premium documents: certificates, welcome books, annual reports.

Iconography

Interface and print icons are drawn as 1.5 px hairline strokes, geometric, with rounded terminals — matching Jost’s construction. Icons are single-colour only: gold on navy, navy on ivory. Filled, duotone or illustrative icons are prohibited. The crown and shield are never simplified into icons; the emblem asset is used instead.

Section 11

Photography & Art Direction

Subject

Real moments of companionship: a walk in Frederiksberg Have, coffee poured properly, hands held at a piano. Clients are photographed upright, engaged and dignified — never from above, never in clinical settings, never as passive recipients. Carers appear in uniform, present but not dominant.

Light & Grade

Natural Nordic light: soft, directional, honest. The grade is warm in the highlights and cool navy in the shadows — echoing gold on midnight. Muted saturation; skin tones true; blacks lifted toward navy, never crushed.

Composition

Generous negative space for typography; eye-level or slightly low camera; interiors that read as homes — books, textiles, daylight — not facilities. Details are welcome: a wedding ring, a chessboard, the fold of a well-made bed.

Prohibited

Stock-photo cheerfulness, white-coat imagery, medical apparatus as hero subject, downward angles on seated elders, heavy HDR, filters, and any image in which a client appears distressed or anonymous without consent.

Tone of Voice — Dansk & English

Premium Care speaks like a trusted family adviser: warm, precise and unhurried. Danish is the first language of the brand; English follows the same register for international families and partners.

PrincipleWe writeWe never write
De-form respect“Vi glæder os til at byde Dem velkommen.” Formal De to clients and families by default; du only when invited.Forced informality, exclamation marks, emoji in client correspondence.
Precision as care“Deres faste hjælper, Mette, kommer tirsdag kl. 9.00.” Names, times and commitments stated exactly.“A member of our team will visit at some point in the morning.”
Dignity first“Støtte til hverdagen” — support for daily life.“The elderly”, “patients”, “beds”, or any language of burden or decline.

Accessibility & Contrast Compliance

The brand serves elderly clients and their families; legibility is an ethical requirement, not a preference. All client-facing text meets WCAG 2.2 AA as a minimum; core reading paths target AAA.

Digital. Body text ≥ 16 px; touch targets ≥ 44 px; visible focus rings in Gold Light; motion respects prefers-reduced-motion; every image of the mark carries the alt text “Premium Care”.
