Section 00 · Foundation

The Document & Its Authority

This is the single source of visual and verbal truth for Premium Care. Every future designer, developer, marketer, photographer, filmmaker, AI model and employee derives their work from this system.

What this system governs

The Brand Operating System is not a style guide to be consulted occasionally. It is the operating layer beneath every artefact the institution produces — from a business card to a vehicle livery, from an investor deck to an AI-generated photograph. Where a question of appearance, tone or reproduction arises, the answer is here. Where it is not, the answer is escalated to the Brand Custodian, never improvised.

The system refines, expands and unifies the existing Premium Care identity. It does not replace the approved mark, the palette, the typography or the doctrine already ratified in the Brand Bible. It professionalises them into one coherent, production-ready standard.

The document hierarchy

DocumentGoverns

This Operating SystemThe complete identity, taught and applied — what & how
Master Implementation SpecProduction tolerance — exactly how it is reproduced
Rule of precedence

In any conflict, the Brand Bible prevails on doctrine; the Implementation Specification prevails on production tolerance; this Operating System prevails on everything between.

The Five Design Tests

Every artefact — before it leaves the building, goes live, or is generated — must pass all five. A single failure sends the work back.

TEST 01

Dignity

Does it honour the person in our care as a person first? Nothing frail, pitiable, or patronising survives.

TEST 02

Restraint

Is every element necessary? If one could be removed without loss, it is removed. Quiet, never flashy.

TEST 03

Faglighed

Is it technically flawless — kerning, contrast, alignment, reproduction? Amateur execution breaks trust.

TEST 04

Consistency

Would it be recognised as Premium Care with the logo removed? The system, not the mark alone, is the brand.

TEST 05

Tryghed

Does it produce the feeling of security — predictable, calm, unsurprising in the reassuring sense?

Section 01 · The Brand

Brand Philosophy

We do not sell care. We preserve dignity. Everything that follows — every colour, letterform and photograph — exists to make that preservation visible.

PURPOSE

Why we exist

To raise the standard of home care in Denmark so that the person receiving care in their own living room is met with the same excellence, discretion and attention to detail found in the nation's finest institutions.

MISSION

What we do

To compose care — planned with clinical rigour, performed with human warmth, and carried out with the quiet confidence of true professionals — in the home, every day.

VISION

Where we are going

To become the standard against which home care in Denmark is measured: the provider families request by name, municipalities trust by record, and caregivers aspire to join.

PROMISE

What clients can count on

Omsorg · Tryghed · Hver Dag. Care, security, every day. The same faces. The agreed times. The promised standard. This is a contract, not a slogan.

Core beliefs

Care in the home is not a compromise

It is the highest expression of dignity — life continued on one's own terms, in one's own rooms, uninterrupted.

Warmth and competence are inseparable

Warmth without competence is sentiment; competence without warmth is service. We insist on both, always.

Trust is built through predictability

Not through claims. The same carers, the agreed times, the transparent invoice — reliability is our luxury.

We position above, never against

Denmark's public care system is a partner and a client, not a foil. We never disparage. We simply hold a higher standard.

The four values

Our values are expressed first in Danish, because they are lived in Danish — in homes, in conversations, in care plans. The English definitions exist so every partner, agency and supplier understands precisely what each value demands of their work.

If Premium Care were a person, they would be a senior Danish clinician of impeccable standing: precise but never cold, warm but never familiar, confident enough to be quiet. They wear one good watch, not three. They arrive on time. They remember your mother's name.

Brand manifesto

How a society treats its elderly and its vulnerable is the truest measure of its character. We exist to raise that measure — one home, one morning, one kept promise at a time.

Tone of voice

To families

"We will be there at eight, as always. And we will stay as long as your mother needs us."

To municipalities

"Our documentation is complete, our compliance record is public, and our pricing contains no surprises."

To colleagues

Writing style — the five rules

  1. Write to one person. Not "clients receive", but "you receive". Not "relatives can", but "your family can".
  2. Short sentences carry trust. If a sentence needs a second breath, it needs an edit.
  3. Plain words for hard subjects. We say dementia, palliative care, end of life — clearly, gently, without euphemism. Euphemism signals discomfort; clarity signals experience.
  4. Never sell fear. We speak to the solution, never dramatise the problem.
  5. Danish dignity in every language. British English for corporate and international; Danish for clients, care documentation and municipal work. The strapline stays Danish everywhere, without exception.
Approved master messages
  • Denmark's highest standard of care, at home.
  • The same faces. The agreed times. The promised standard.
  • Care planned like medicine. Delivered like family. (private audiences)
  • Premium standards. Procurement-grade transparency. (municipal)
  • Your home. Your routine. Your life. We fit around you.
  • Work where the standard matches your pride. (recruitment)
Words never to use
  • world-class · solutions · passion / passionate
  • journey (metaphorical) · loved ones · holistic
  • wellness journey · empowerment · synergy
  • Superlatives without evidence ("best in Denmark")
  • Fear-based messaging ("Don't let your parents suffer alone")
  • Price-led messaging ("Affordable care" — we do not compete on price)
The Integrity Ledger

Clinical and human integrity hold structural veto power over commercial decisions. Where a message would sell more but honour the person less, the person wins. This is not a preference; it is the brand's constitution.

Colourway doctrine

Construction & clear space

The construction unit is X = the height of the crown. All proportions, clear space and placement are expressed in X, keeping every rule scale-independent and verifiable with a ruler.

Clear space

X on all four sides, all lockups, no exceptions. Doubled to 2X on transparent substrates (glass, vehicle windows) and cluttered photographic grounds. Nothing enters clear space: no type, rules, icons, folds, seams, stitching or trim edges.

Optical corrections — never "fix" them

  • Shield side walls carry a 1.5° entasis (barely perceptible swell) — flattening makes the shield look pinched.
  • The cross bar sits 2% above geometric centre — centring reads as drooping.
  • Wordmark letterspacing is optically kerned in the master; type may never be re-set from fonts.

Minimum sizes

Below VT minimum, the strapline drops automatically. Below absolute minimum, the mark is not used.

Scaling & placement rules

  1. Proportional scaling only. Non-uniform scaling, rotation, skew or perspective is prohibited.
  2. Vector masters scale infinitely upward; downward, the minimums above are hard floors.
  3. At 16–48 px, only pixel-fitted micro-masters (FV, small SH) may be used. Scaling print vectors to favicon sizes produces mush; mush violates Faglighed.
  4. Large-format (> 2 m): scale from vector, then review the entasis and stroke weights on proof plot before fabrication.
  5. On photography, place in a verified 4.5:1 local-contrast zone — bottom-left or bottom-right, over consistent tone (sky, wall, floor, uniform). Never over faces, hands, or complex patterns.

Incorrect usage — twelve prohibitions

Never do this
  • Stretch, condense or distort
  • Recolour outside approved colourways
  • Rotate or tilt
  • Add shadows, glows or effects
  • Place full colour on red or busy grounds
  • Crowd the clear space or add typography
Never do this either
  • Redraw or re-proportion any element
  • Outline the mark or apply gradients
  • Place the emblem inside other shapes
  • Animate individual elements separately
  • Reconstruct lockups at non-approved spacing
  • Use below the minimum size

Source-of-truth doctrine

Vector is the source of truth. Every raster file is an export, regenerated from the master — never opened and edited. A file without a version number is unreleased by definition.

Colour psychology — why these four

Navy

The colour of institutions that endure — law, medicine, the state. It signals authority without aggression, seriousness without coldness. It is our black precisely because black would read as fashion, not trust.

Gold

Craft and worth, used with restraint. Gold in quantity is ostentation; gold as a hairline is a jeweller's confidence. It marks the people we serve as the treasure of the institution.

Red

Porcelain

Not white — porcelain. A warm, near-white with the faintest cream, the colour of good paper and clean linen. It gives every element room to breathe: Nærvær, made visible.

Section 04 · Typography

Type System

Three voices, one system. The serif speaks with heritage, the sans works without fuss, the mono renders data with clinical precision. Together they are the brand's speaking voice on the page.

The three typefaces

VOICE
Aa

Serif — Berlingske Serif

Headlines, pull-quotes, the wordmark's spirit. Danish heritage in letterform. Used with restraint; never for long body text.

Screen / web substitute: Cormorant Garamond. Office fallback: Georgia → Times New Roman.

WORK
Aa

Sans — Berlingske Sans

Body text, UI, labels, navigation — the largest text volume. Humanist warmth at small sizes; the brand's working voice.

Screen / web substitute: Jost. Office fallback: Verdana → Arial.

DATA
Aa

Mono — Berlingske Mono

Numerals, tables, code, specifications, file names. Clinical precision; every figure aligns.

Screen / web substitute: IBM Plex Mono. Office fallback: Courier New → Consolas.

Why the fallback matters

A municipal caseworker opening a Word document without brand fonts must still receive a dignified, legible page. Georgia and Verdana were designed for screen legibility at small sizes — the best available substitutes for Berlingske's humanist warmth. The fallback stack is non-negotiable for tenders and Office templates.

Danish characters, numerals & micro-rules

Danish characters

æ ø å   Æ Ø Å

Every typeface in the system must render æ, ø and å with full fidelity. A substitute font that mangles Danish diacritics is disqualified, however handsome its Latin set.

Numerals

Old-style figures in running prose (they sit like lowercase and read as text); tabular lining figures in tables, invoices and financial data (they align in columns). Never mix the two in one context.

Micro-typographic rules

  • The interpunct · is gold and set with a thin space either side — it is the brand's signature separator (as in the strapline).
  • Left-aligned, ragged-right throughout. Justified text is never used — it creates rivers and reads as bureaucratic.
  • One space after a full stop. Curly quotes and proper apostrophes always; straight quotes never.
  • En-dash for ranges, em-dash for parenthetical breaks, hyphen only for compounds.
  • Body 9.5 pt minimum in print; client-facing clinical materials 11 pt+.

Section 05 · Composition

Layout System

The page is composed the way a care visit is planned: with a clear structure, generous room, and nothing left to chance. Whitespace is not empty — it is Nærvær.

The grid

All Premium Care layouts sit on a 12-column grid with a consistent baseline rhythm. Twelve divides cleanly into halves, thirds, quarters and sixths — every composition the institution needs, without improvised columns.

Whitespace & visual rhythm

The 8-point spacing scale

All spacing is a multiple of 8 (4 for fine adjustments): 4 · 8 · 16 · 24 · 32 · 48 · 64 · 96 · 128. Nothing sits at an arbitrary distance. Consistent spacing is what the eye reads as "considered".

Generosity is the default

When in doubt, add space, not elements. A Premium Care page is never crowded. Margins are wide, line-length is comfortable, and every block is given room to be understood before the next begins.

Composition principles

  1. One idea per view. A slide, a page, a screen carries a single argument. Supporting detail is subordinate and visibly so.
  2. Left-anchored. Text aligns left; the eye starts where reading starts. Centred text is reserved for the cover, the strapline, and closing statements.
  3. The gold keyline as punctuation. A single hairline gold rule marks a section boundary or the top edge of a signature panel — never decoration, always structure.
  4. Asymmetry with balance. Compositions are asymmetric (a wide text column, a narrow image column) but optically balanced. Symmetry is used sparingly, for gravity.
  5. Photography earns its place. An image is full-bleed or precisely inset to the grid — never floated awkwardly. It illustrates, it does not fill.
The signature panel

The recurring Premium Care layout device: a navy panel with a single gold keyline along its top edge, holding a serif headline and a short sans paragraph. It appears on covers, section dividers, slides and web heroes — the visual equivalent of the strapline.

Section 06 · Image

Photography Direction

Our photography shows people as people — upright, present, at eye level. We never photograph frailty as the point. The Danish home is a place worth staying in, and we light it that way.

The three approved categories

01 · RELATIONAL

People, between equals

Carer and client at eye level, in genuine exchange. The relationship is companionship, never supervision. Warmth in the eyes, ease in the posture.

02 · ENVIRONMENTAL

The Danish home

Lived-in rooms with character — worn wooden floors, coffee cups, books, gardens. Morning light through windows. A place worth staying in, not a showcase.

03 · PROFESSIONAL

Competence, visible

Documentation, clinical rigour, the uniform, the considered hand. Faglighed made photographic — precise, never sterile.

Lighting & colour grade

Lighting

  • Natural light only. Early morning or late afternoon — the Nordic golden hour.
  • Overcast days preferred: soft, even, flattering to every age of skin.
  • No artificial lighting unless it is the home's own existing light.
  • No hard shadows, no flash, no studio strobe on faces.

Colour grade

  • Warm-neutral, slightly desaturated. Skin tones true and healthy.
  • Highlights soft, never blown; shadows open, never crushed.
  • A faint warmth in the whites — porcelain, not clinical blue-white.
  • Consistent grade across an entire set: the LUT is part of the brand.

Composition, lenses & depth of field

ParameterStandard
Lenses35 mm & 50 mm primes (relational) · 24–35 mm (environmental) · 85 mm (portrait)
Depth of fieldShallow-to-moderate; subject sharp, context softly present. Never so shallow the room disappears.
Aspect ratios3:2 · 4:5 · 16:9 · 1:1 — approved set only
CompositionEye-level camera height; room shown, not just furniture; subject given air; rule-of-thirds, never dead-centre.

Rejected photography

Never use
  • Anyone appearing frail as the point of the image
  • The carer positioned above or over the client
  • Hands clasped in "comfort" clichés
  • Personal care moments (also an ethics breach)
Never use
  • Stock-photo interiors — catalogue-perfect, no life
  • Clinical or hospital-like settings (unless a genuine "before")
  • Cool, blue-white grades that read as institutional
  • Staged smiles at the camera; forced cheer
Ethics & consent

Every person photographed gives written, informed consent, retained on file. We never photograph a client in a vulnerable moment for marketing. Dignity is not a mood we stage — it is a condition of the shoot.

Image selection workflow

  1. Does it pass the Dignity test? If any doubt, reject.
  2. Is the light natural and the grade on-brand?
  3. Is the relationship between equals?
  4. Is consent on file?
  5. Does the logo, if placed, sit in a verified 4.5:1 contrast zone?

AI image-generation prompt templates

Where photography is generated, it obeys every rule above. Prompts are engineered against the brand grade and never produce a person who could be mistaken for a real, identifiable individual. (The full library is in Section 17.)

Section 07 · Illustration

Illustration System

Illustration is used sparingly, and only where a photograph cannot go — a process, a data relationship, a map. The style is calm, linear and unmistakably part of the heraldic family.

Style

Line, not fill

Premium Care illustration is built from confident single-weight line work with restrained flat fills. It echoes the shield's engraved quality — considered, precise, never cartoonish.

Geometric, humane

Forms are geometrically constructed but softened at terminals, matching the figures in the mark: upright, dignified, never exaggerated.

Infographics, diagrams, charts & maps

Navy tonal scale + one gold accent. No rainbow palettes. Every series labelled directly, never by legend colour alone.

DIAGRAMS

Boxes with 2 px radius, hairline navy strokes, gold connectors for the critical path only.

Line icons + a single large mono statistic. Restraint over density — one insight per unit.

Data-visualisation doctrine

Charts inherit the colour proportion doctrine. The default is a monochrome navy scale; gold marks the one point that matters. If a chart needs more than navy-plus-gold to be understood, it needs to be two charts.

Section 13 · Uniform

Embroidery standards

Threads & technique

  • Polyester thread (Madeira Polyneon or equal), lab-dipped to navy 282 C, non-metallic antique gold matched to 871, red 186 C, optical white.

Section 15 · Motion

Motion Design

Motion in Premium Care is calm and purposeful. It reassures; it never performs. If an animation draws attention to itself, it has failed.

Principles

01

Calm

Ease-out curves, gentle fades and slides. Nothing bounces, spins, or flashes. The pace of a considered visit.

02

Purposeful

Motion communicates state and hierarchy — what changed, what follows. Decorative motion is removed.

03

Respectful

Reduced-motion is always honoured. No motion carries essential meaning; every animation is an enhancement, never a dependency.

Timing & transitions

InteractionDurationEasing
Micro-interaction (hover, focus)120–180 msease-out
Component transition (card, modal)240–320 mscubic-bezier(.4,0,.2,1)
Page / section reveal320–480 msease-out, staggered
Logo animation (video title)≤ 1.2 sfade & settle, never assemble

Logo animation rules

  • The mark fades and settles into place as one object. Its elements are never animated separately — the crown does not fly in, the figures do not walk.
  • A single gold keyline may draw in beneath a title card.
  • No 3D rotation, no shine sweep, no particle effects.

Video & presentation motion

Video grading

Matches the photography grade exactly — warm-neutral, soft highlights, true skin. Title cards on the navy signature panel; lower-thirds in Sans with a gold keyline.

The silent brand

Sound design is minimal to absent. Care is quiet. Where music is used it is restrained and never emotionally manipulative — we do not score sentiment we have not earned.

Transitions

Cuts and gentle cross-dissolves only. No wipes, spins, zooms or flashy transitions. The edit, like the care, is composed.

Section 18 · Assurance

Quality Assurance

Before anything leaves the building, goes live, or is generated, it clears these checklists. Consistency is not an accident of talent — it is the product of a discipline anyone can follow.

The universal pre-flight checklist

  • ✓Passes all Five Design Tests — Dignity, Restraint, Faglighed, Consistency, Tryghed.
  • ✓Correct logo lockup for the context, in an approved colourway, above minimum size, with full clear space.
  • ✓Palette obeys the 60/30/8/2 proportion; gold used once; red only within the shield.
  • ✓Typography from the approved system; fallback stack embedded where fonts are not guaranteed.
  • ✓Layout on the 12-column grid; spacing on the 8-point scale; left-aligned, ragged-right.
  • ✓Every claim evidenced; no forbidden vocabulary; written to one person.
  • ✓Contrast verified, not judged by eye — body ≥ 7:1 target, 4.5:1 floor; no colour-only signals.
  • ✓Danish characters (æ ø å) render correctly; strapline present and in Danish.
  • ✓File named to convention with a version number; exported in the correct format.

Medium-specific checklists

Print

  • ✓PDF/X-4, CMYK + spot, 3 mm bleed, 12 mm safe area, ink ≤ 300%.
  • ✓One speciality finish per item (certificates excepted).
  • ✓Greyscale-verified if the piece may be photocopied.
  • ✓Fogra contract proof for a new supplier's first run.

Digital / web

  • ✓WCAG 2.2 AA; visible keyboard focus; reduced-motion honoured.
  • ✓Logo is vector (SVG); one gold CTA maximum per page.
  • ✓Responsive to mobile; tables and figures legible at 320 px.
  • ✓No invented dark mode — navy is the dark surface.

Uniform / environment

  • ✓Embroidery from DST/EMB master; non-metallic gold thread.
  • ✓Certification outranks branding on safety garments.
